**Summary:** This note summarizes Pranjal K's updated opinion on buying a home, emphasizing affordability, long-term commitment for living, and cautioning against real estate as a primary investment vehicle.

**Finology Opinion for Buying Home by Pranjal K**

The speaker's opinions on real estate have evolved significantly since a previous discussion. Initially, they advised buying pre-launch projects from trusted builders like Tata or Godrej for better pricing and payment plans, assuming these large brands minimized risks.

However, the speaker now acknowledges missing crucial aspects:
*   **Execution Risk:** Even trusted builders can have issues with construction quality, carpet area discrepancies, and post-construction problems like seepage or plumbing faults.
*   **Delay Risk:** Projects, even from reputable builders, can face significant delays, impacting planned move-in dates.

**Updated Opinion on Buying a Home (for living, not investment):**

1.  **Affordability:** Ensure the EMI for the home does not exceed 30-35% of your total family income.
2.  **Long-Term Commitment:** Be certain you want to live in that specific city, locality, and project for the next 20-30 years. This is crucial because renting has its own set of problems (landlord issues, frequent moves, address changes, furniture damage).
3.  **Land Appreciation vs. Building Depreciation:** Understand that in real estate, the land appreciates, while the building depreciates. The value of the flat itself will likely decrease relative to newer, more modern constructions. When calculating future value, consider that only the land's proportional share will appreciate significantly, while the building's value will likely remain stagnant or decrease.

**Investment Perspective:**
The speaker is largely against real estate as an investment, citing capital lock-in, maintenance costs, and mental load. They believe simpler investment options like gold or ETFs offer better liquidity and comparable returns with less hassle. They are gradually exiting their real estate investments.

**Recommendation:**
Buy a home if you need a place to live and can meet the affordability and long-term commitment criteria. Do not buy solely for investment purposes.

The speaker hopes this updated opinion remains consistent for several years.
