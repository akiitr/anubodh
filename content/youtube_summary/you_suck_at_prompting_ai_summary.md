# Video Summary: You SUCK at Prompting AI (Here's the secret)

**Source:** [You SUCK at Prompting AI (Here's the secret)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwWBcsxEoLk)

**Summary:**
The video explains that effective AI prompting is a skill that involves programming the AI rather than just asking questions. It emphasizes that LLMs are prediction engines, and good prompts guide these predictions by providing clear context, personas, and output requirements. The core techniques discussed are:
    1.  **Understanding LLMs as Prediction Engines:** They don't "think" but complete patterns based on statistical probability.
    2.  **Personas:** Assigning a role or expertise to the AI to focus its knowledge base (e.g., "You are a senior site reliability engineer"). This is often part of the "system prompt" in API-based interactions but can be included in the user prompt.
    3.  **Context:** Providing detailed, specific information to the AI to reduce guesswork and hallucinations. The principle is "always be contexting" (ABC).
    4.  **Tools and Web Search:** Enabling AIs to use external tools like web search to overcome their knowledge cut-off and access real-time information. This requires caution as the AI might use unreliable sources.
    5.  **Allowing AI to Fail:** Explicitly telling the AI it's okay to say "I don't know" if information is not available or understood, which helps prevent hallucinations.
    6.  **Output Requirements:** Clearly defining the desired format, tone, length, and style of the AI's response. This can range from professional to highly specific, even humorous.
    7.  **Few-Shot Prompting:** Providing examples of desired input-output pairs to teach the AI a specific pattern or style, which is more effective than just describing the output.

The video uses an example of improving a CloudFlare apology email to demonstrate these techniques, transforming a generic, soulless message into a detailed, transparent, and appropriately toned communication. It stresses that providing context is paramount and that LLMs rarely admit ignorance unless explicitly permitted.

**Key Points:**
*   Prompting is programming, not just asking questions.
*   LLMs are prediction engines, not conscious thinkers.
*   Use personas to focus the AI's knowledge.
*   Provide ample context to prevent hallucinations.
*   Enable tools like web search for up-to-date information, but be wary of sources.
*   Grant the AI permission to say "I don't know" to combat hallucinations.
*   Define clear output requirements for format, tone, and style.
*   Few-shot prompting (providing examples) is highly effective.
*   "Always be contexting" (ABC).
